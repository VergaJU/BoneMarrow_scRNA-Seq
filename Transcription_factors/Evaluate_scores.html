Difference or similarity between the scored TF in resident and exhausted cells¶

From scenic we have 2 set of scored TFs. Now to evaluate if they are indeed similar of different, I am going to do some exploratory analysis, then evaluate more robustly which is their similarity.

I will start with:

  • plotting their distribution and the top enriched TFs
  • Plot the overlap of 10% top eriched TF
  • Evaluate scored TF between resident and exhausted NK cells using Rank Biased Overlap (RBO). This method seems adapt for this comparison because:
    • Can evaluate different sizes of ranked lists
    • It can assign weight to the n top ranks
    • provide a ration (or percentage) of similarity

Conclusions¶

The top 5 enriched TFs are similar. Of them:

  • STAT1, STAT2, IRF7, IRF9, and their regulons are mainly involved in the response to immune stimulus (interferon, MHC, cytokine)
  • REL in exhausted NK cell looks involved in reduced apoptosis
  • CREM in resident NK cells looks more involved in mRNA regulation and ubiquitination

Increasing the comparison to the top 10% enriched TFs only 9 are shared by the two group of cells. Finally analysing the similarity of the two ranked list of TFs, results suggest only a partial similarity, the highest score is ot the 73% giving 90% of the weight to the top 10 genes. Increasing the number of TFs with high weiht or reducing their weight the similarity score drops quickly to ~60%. Considering only the top 10% enriched TF the scores are slightly similar. Considering the similarity between the 2 cell types, I think the result are expected and the differences could provide more insights about NK exhaustion.
